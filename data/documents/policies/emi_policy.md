# EMI Payment Policy

## What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month.

## EMI Calculation

EMI is calculated using the reducing balance method, which considers:
- Principal amount
- Interest rate
- Loan tenure

## Payment Methods

You can pay your EMI through:

1. Auto-debit from savings account
2. Standing instructions
3. Online payment via mobile app or internet banking
4. Branch payment
5. NEFT/RTGS transfer

## Payment Due Date

- EMI due date is mentioned in your loan agreement
- Payment should be made by the due date to avoid penalties
- Grace period: 3 days after due date

## Missed EMI Consequences

If you miss an EMI payment:

- Late payment charges will be levied
- Your credit score may be affected
- After 3 consecutive missed EMIs, loan may be classified as NPA
- Legal action may be initiated for recovery

## EMI Bounced

If your EMI bounces due to insufficient funds:

- Bounced cheque charges will be levied
- Bank charges may apply
- You must make the payment immediately

## Prepayment

You can prepay your loan:
- Partial prepayment allowed
- Full prepayment allowed
- Prepayment charges may apply (check your loan agreement)
